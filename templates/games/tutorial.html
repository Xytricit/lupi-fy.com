Lupi-fy is a drag-and-drop game creation platform. You can build 2D games entirely in your browser without coding. This tutorial will walk you through creating your first game.

Step 1: Start the Editor

Click the button below to open the game editor. This is where you'll design your game visually.

Open Editor

Step 2: Give Your Game a Title

At the top of the editor, you'll see an input field. Type in a creative name for your game (e.g., "Flappy Bird Clone", "Dodge The Enemies").

My Awesome Game

Step 3: Build Logic with Blocks

The editor has a block panel on the right side. Drag blocks into the workspace to define game behavior:

  • On Start: What happens when the game loads?
  • On Collision: What happens when two objects touch?
  • On Key Press: What happens when the player presses a key?
  • Move Sprite: Move a character or object.
  • Change Health: Increase or decrease health points.
  • Destroy Sprite: Remove an object from the game.
  • Spawn Sprite: Create a new object.

Example: Drag "On Key Press" (for "arrow_left"), then drag "Move Sprite" underneath to move the player left when the arrow key is pressed.

Step 4: Preview Your Game

Click the "Run Preview" button to test your game in the canvas on the left. You'll see your logic in action!

Step 5: Save Your Game

Click "Save" to store your game. You can save multiple versions and come back to edit them anytime. Your game is saved as a Draft until you publish it.

Step 6: Publish Your Game

Once you're happy with your game, click "Publish" to submit it for review. A moderator will review it and approve it for the public to play.

Review Criteria: No offensive content, no malware, playable and fun!

üí° Pro Tips

  • Start simple: make a single-level game with one mechanic (jump, avoid, collect).
  • Test often: use "Run Preview" to check your game works as expected.
  • Add feedback: use "Change Health" or "Spawn Sprite" to give players visual feedback.
  • Name objects clearly: use names like "player", "enemy", "coin" to avoid confusion.
  • Think about win/lose conditions: how does the player win or lose?
